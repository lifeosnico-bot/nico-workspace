## What happens after lis pendens is filed?

## Short Answer:

This critical barrier can disrupt property sales and forewarn of involved legal proceedings. Our concise overview will clarify the subsequent steps—from the impact on property transactions to potential legal outcomes and how parties involved can navigate the resolution process. Stay informed and poised to handle the ramifications of a lis pendens with this direct and practical guide.

## Key Takeaways

- A lis pendens has a noticeable impact on property sales by serving as a public notice that a property’s title is in question due to pending litigation, which can deter potential buyers and affect the property’s market value.
- There are legal consequences for misfiling a lis pendens such as potential sanctions, invalidation, and possibly damages for slander of title, therefore strict compliance with statutory requirements and proper filing practices are essential.
- Removal of a lis pendens can be achieved through a court-ordered expungement or voluntary discharge by the filer, both of which require specific legal procedures and documentation to clear the property title for future transactions.

## The Aftermath of Filing a Lis Pendens

![Real estate sign with blurred background](https://montague.law/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/cf3afc70-e85e-4add-97b0-742ed45629cc.png)

Imagine you’re interested in purchasing a charming cottage by the lake, only to discover that a lis pendens has been filed against it. You’d probably think twice about proceeding with the purchase, wouldn’t you? That’s precisely the ripple effect a lis pendens can have on property sales. It signals a pending lawsuit involving the property, and it can hang like a sword of Damocles over any potential transactions. In essence, a lis pendens is a beacon of impending legal issues that can deter potential buyers and impact the property’s marketability.

However, there’s a flip side to the coin. The filing of a lis pendens is not to be taken lightly, as it comes with possible legal consequences. Compliance with statutory requirements while filing is imperative to prevent potential invalidity and associated liabilities. Once recorded against the property, it can even grant priority to the party who filed the notice as of the recording date. This can affect subsequent property interests and potentially spark legal disputes regarding the sufficiency of the notice in the context of the pending legal action.

### Impact on Property Sale

![Potential buyers visiting a property](https://montague.law/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/f8231013-85bf-44a9-9d23-5cd84781306c.png)

The filing of a lis pendens effectively raises a red flag for potential buyers and lenders. This serves to notify them that the property’s title is in question due to a pending lawsuit, thereby reducing its appeal. For example, consider a divorce scenario where one spouse files a lis pendens to protect their interest in the marital home. This action would alert any potential buyers that the other spouse cannot sell the property until the divorce is settled and the property’s ownership is clarified.

Of course, the impact on the property’s market value is significant as well. The lis pendens becomes a matter of public notice, deterring potential buyers and leading to a decrease in the property’s market value. Despite the typical reduction in buyer interest due to a lis pendens, certain buyers may still choose to proceed with the purchase. These may include those with the financial capability to buy the property outright or those willing to navigate the related legal complexities.

### Legal Consequences

![Legal documents with gavel in the background](https://montague.law/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/00aef42a-1a60-451c-808d-a9cb1a319f2d.png)

While a lis pendens can serve as a protective shield for a party’s claim to a property, misuse or mishandling of the notice can lead to serious legal repercussions. These could range from slander of title to exposure to consequential damages. A lis pendens serves as a constructive notice to potential buyers and lenders, and its misuse can lead to legal issues that go beyond the property dispute.

Should a lis pendens notice possess technical defects or be filed in an unfamiliar jurisdiction, it may be set aside. This might result in both the attorney and client facing sanctions, with the matter potentially escalated to a superior court for further review and resolution. Moreover, excessive use of a lis pendens notice may lead to damages for slander of title, which can affect both real and personal property.

## The Resolution Process

The turbulent waters of a lis pendens dispute pose a challenge. However, established procedures exist to resolve these pending action cases. These involve:

1. Recording a notice of pendency of action
2. Initiating a lawsuit related to the property
3. Seeking dismissal if the action is resolved
4. Potentially removing or expunging the lis pendens.

The judge holds an important role in this process, supervising the legal proceedings and making judgments on the validity and removal of the lis pendens. The judge’s decision, based on the evidence and arguments put forth by the parties, can greatly influence the outcome of the lis pendens and the resolution of the underlying dispute. However, it’s not always about the gavel and the courtroom. There’s also room for negotiation and settlement.

### Litigation Outcome

A lawsuit related to the property initiates the litigation process in a lis pendens case. Once the suit is pending in court, a party involved in the case can officially record a lis pendens. This functions as a beacon, alerting anyone interested in the property about the ongoing lawsuit and its implications for the property.

The outcome of litigation in such cases can be impacted by a variety of factors, including:

- The content of the lis pendens notice
- Compliance with statutory requirements
- The type of lawsuit in question
- Correct service of notice
- The merits of the underlying lawsuit

All of these factors play a role in determining the litigation outcome.

If the litigation validates the lis pendens, it affirms the existence of a valid pending lawsuit under the common law doctrine that could affect the ownership or other rights associated with the property.

### Negotiated Settlements

Although litigation can be complex and drawn-out, it is not the sole method to resolve a lis pendens dispute. There’s also the option of negotiated settlements. These are typically structured through settlement negotiations with the party who filed the lis pendens, or through dismissal or a court judgment in favor of one party.

Negotiated settlements aim to resolve the underlying dispute that led to the lis pendens filing. They often involve terms like quiet title actions, partition lawsuits, and specific performance, all aimed at reaching an agreement that serves the interests of all parties involved.

## Removing a Lis Pendens

Once a lis pendens has been filed, property sales are affected and parties have weathered litigation or negotiation. What follows this? Removal of the lis pendens. This can be achieved either through a court order or voluntarily by the party who filed it, effectively clearing the property for transactions to proceed.

However, the removal of a lis pendens isn’t exclusive to the parties directly involved in the lawsuit. According to the law, any party with a vested interest can submit a motion to release the notice, provided they meet specific criteria. Now let’s delve into the specifics of removing a lis pendens.

### Court-Ordered Removal

A court can order the removal of a lis pendens if it finds the notice to be invalid or the dispute resolved. This procedure is known as ‘expungement’. The real property owner can submit a motion to the court to expunge the lis pendens, provided they can demonstrate that the claimant lacks a valid real property claim in their underlying lawsuit.

The process of securing a court-ordered removal includes filing a motion for expungement. This can be pursued upon prevailing on a real property claim associated with the lis pendens or upon establishing the invalidity of the lis pendens. And while this might sound like a daunting task, it’s worth noting that the court provides a recommended format for the necessary documentation, including an application, order, and summons.

### Voluntary Discharge

On the other hand, the party who filed the lis pendens can choose to voluntarily discharge it. This could be due to a resolution of the dispute or a decision to no longer assert their claim on the property. The voluntary discharge process involves:

1. Filing a ‘Notice of Voluntary Dismissal and Release’
2. Obtaining a certificate of release from the court clerk
3. Recording it with the title company to formally clear the notice.

While this might seem like a straightforward process, it’s important to be aware of the potential costs involved, which may include attorney’s fees and other associated charges. Furthermore, it’s crucial to understand the potential implications on the claimant’s legal rights, as voluntary discharge can bring an end to the legal proceeding and remove the notice from the land records.

## Protecting Your Interests

Whether you’re a property owner caught in a lis pendens dispute or a potential buyer considering a property with a lis pendens, protecting your interests should be your top priority. This involves two key aspects: seeking legal representation and conducting due diligence. These elements can safeguard your rights and interests, ensuring you’re adequately prepared to navigate the complexities of a lis pendens situation.

### Legal Representation

The involvement of a competent real estate attorney can significantly influence the outcome of a lis pendens dispute. Having a legal expert by your side can help you navigate the intricacies of the process, from filing and recording a lis pendens to clearing a wrongful lis pendens from the property and pursuing damages.

When selecting a real estate attorney, consider the following factors:

- Their experience in real estate law and litigation
- Their understanding of lis pendens case management
- Their familiarity with the laws and regulations in the relevant jurisdiction

A real estate attorney should have obtained a bachelor’s degree and a juris doctor degree from an accredited law school, demonstrating the formal education necessary for legal practice in real estate.

### Due Diligence

Another crucial element in protecting your interest subject lies in due diligence. This involves:

- A comprehensive investigation of the physical and financial condition of the property
- Review of documents
- Conducting inspections
- Evaluation of risks
- Understanding of property disclosures.

By conducting a thorough title, tax, and lis pendens search, you can uncover any possible liens or concerns related to the property. This ensures you’re fully informed about any legal claims or issues associated with the property, helping you to make informed decisions and minimize the likelihood of encountering issues related to a lis pendens.

## Preventing Lis Pendens

Although understanding how to navigate a lis pendens dispute is crucial, the ancient wisdom still rings true: it’s better to prevent than to cure. So, how can you prevent a lis pendens? The answer lies in clear communication and proper documentation. These two elements can significantly reduce the likelihood of disputes or claims that could result in a lis pendens being filed.

### Clear Communication

Any real estate transaction requires clear and transparent communication, especially when they involve real property. It helps confirm the absence of any legal issues or defects in the legal title of the property being purchased, reducing the chance of disputes or claims that could result in a lis pendens.

Moreover, open communication enables property managers to promptly address conflicts, mediate disputes, and find mutually beneficial solutions. This prevents disputes from escalating and facilitates a smoother resolution process.

By offering information to potential buyers or lenders regarding the ongoing litigation, you can stop them from obtaining a stake in the property without awareness of the legal matters.

### Proper Documentation

Another crucial facet in avoiding a lis pendens is proper documentation. This involves ensuring all agreements, contracts, and other relevant documents are properly documented and legally binding. It helps guarantee the accurate and correct filing of the lis pendens, establishes a legitimate claim against the property title, and specifically pertains to the property involved in the lawsuit.

Moreover, common errors in document preparation, such as inaccurate property descriptions, use of improper names, and issues related to shared property, can lead to disagreements and potential lis pendens. By ensuring these errors are avoided, you can significantly reduce the risk of a lis pendens.

## Summary

From understanding the aftermath of filing a lis pendens to navigating the complex resolution process, removing a lis pendens, and protecting your interests, we’ve covered a lot of ground. Remember, clear communication, proper documentation, and legal representation are your best allies when it comes to preventing and handling a lis pendens.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the steps for lis pendens?

To put a property on lis pendens in most states, you need to file a lawsuit with the county clerk and have the suit pending, followed by recording the land in the county land records to attach it to the property’s title. This process may vary by state.

### What is the time period when a lis pendens is effective?

A lis pendens is effective as long as the legal action is pending, but it can be withdrawn or expunged by a court motion. However, caution should still be exercised even if the lis pendens is removed.

### What happens in a quiet title action?

In a quiet title action, a lawsuit is filed to confirm ownership, fix title errors, or settle ownership disputes. The end result is a court order clarifying who owns the property. This legal action can remove any clouds on the title and protect the owner from further challenges to their title.

### What is the statute of lis pendens in Texas?

In Texas, the statute of lis pendens allows the party seeking relief to file a Lis Pendens during the pendency of an action involving title to real property, the establishment of an interest in real property, or the enforcement of an encumbrance against real property.

### What are examples of lis pendens?

Examples of lis pendens include disputes over the enforceability of a letter of intent for real estate acquisition, divorce cases involving property division, and foreclosure cases. Lis pendens notices are commonly used in these types of legal disputes.
